Welcome to CognaSync

Here's a quick overview of what you can do in the platform before your first login.

Goal 1

Get oriented on your patient panel

Your dashboard shows each patient's last check-in date, 30-day mood and stress averages, medication adherence, and color-coded alerts — 🔴 urgent, 🟡 watch, 🔵 informational. Start with the flagged rows.

Goal 2

Prepare for an appointment

Open a patient's detail page and click Start Appointment Session. Generate an AI clinical summary, review AI-suggested interview questions drawn from the patient's actual data, read shared journal entries, and take notes — all in one workspace. Everything autosaves.

Goal 3

Go deeper on trends

The trends view shows time-series charts for mood, sleep, stress, energy, and adherence. The medication timing panel shows average time taken and variability per medication when the patient logs event times.

Goal 4

Respond to a crisis flag

A 🔴 crisis indicator means the patient's entry contained explicit crisis language. The patient was shown crisis resources only — no AI output. After following up directly, resolve the flag from their detail page.

Welcome to CognaSync

Here's what to do in your first few days to get the most out of the platform.

Step 1

Complete your first check-in

Log your mood, stress, sleep, medications, and any notes. Takes about two minutes. You'll get a brief AI reflection after each one — your actual numbers, in plain language.

Step 2

Write your first journal entry

Write whatever's on your mind. Entries are shared with your provider by default — toggle that off before saving if you want to keep one private.

Step 3

Set up your medication list

Add your medications in Settings. Once they're there, you can log doses with a single tap from the home screen — no need to open a full check-in.

Step 4

Link your provider

Go to Settings → Provider and enter your provider's CognaSync email. Once linked, they'll have read access to your data. You can unlink at any time.

Step 5

Generate a pre-appointment summary

A few days before your next appointment, go to Pre-Appt Summary and generate one. It covers the past 14 days and suggests specific things to bring up with your provider.
