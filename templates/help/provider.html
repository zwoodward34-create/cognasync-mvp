Getting started with CognaSync

Structured behavioral data from your patients between sessions — surfaced before you walk into the room.

Goal 1

Get oriented on your patient panel

When you log in, you land on the Provider Dashboard. Each row is one patient. At a glance you can see their last check-in date, 30-day mood and stress averages, medication adherence rate, and any active alerts.

Alert levels:

🔴 Urgent — statistically significant declining trend (R² ≥ 0.25, p ≤ 0.05) or active crisis signal. Review immediately.
🟡 Watch — elevated stress trend, medication adherence below 80%, or sustained Crash Risk. Monitor at next session.
🔵 Informational — notable pattern, no threshold crossed. Good context for the appointment.

A patient row with no alerts and a recent check-in is stable. Start with the flagged ones.

Goal 2

Prepare for an appointment

This is the primary workflow. From a patient's detail page, click Start Appointment Session. The workspace gives you:

AI Clinical Summary — generated from the patient's check-ins and shared journals over the selected review window. Click Generate Summary to produce it. It covers mood, stress, sleep, and energy averages with trend direction; medication adherence and timing; advanced data averages (exercise, social quality, workload friction) when available; qualitative journal themes; and flagged threshold crossings.

AI-Suggested Questions — 3–5 interview questions derived from this patient's specific numbers (e.g., "Mood has averaged 3.8/10 this period — what's been contributing to that?"). Starting points, not scripts.

Shared journals — entries the patient chose to share, scoped to the appointment window.

Notes and care plan — fields for session notes, guided Q&A, action items, and care plan updates. All autosave. Set a next appointment date to close the session cleanly.

Click Complete Appointment when done. This closes the workspace and saves the record to the patient's appointment history.
Goal 3

Go deeper on a patient

From a patient's detail page, click View Trends to open the full trend chart view. Charts cover mood, sleep, stress, energy, and medication adherence. Adjust the lookback window between 7 and 90 days.

The medication timing panel shows average time taken per medication and timing variability (standard deviation in minutes) when the patient has been logging event times. Variability above 60 minutes across 7+ days is surfaced as a pattern — it's observational, not a clinical warning.

Interaction flags appear on the detail page if the patient's medication list contains combinations with known signals. These are reference flags, not clinical advisories.

Goal 4

Respond to a crisis flag

A 🔴 crisis indicator means a recent check-in or journal entry contained language matching crisis detection criteria — explicit statements of self-harm or suicidal ideation.

When this flag is active: the patient was shown only the static crisis resource block, no AI-generated content was returned to them, and the entry was still saved so you can review it.

After you have followed up with the patient directly, open their detail page and click Resolve Crisis Flag. This marks the flag as addressed — it does not delete the underlying entry.

Do not resolve the flag before making contact with the patient.
Reference

What the AI does and doesn't do

What it does: describes patterns in numbers. Average mood over a period. Trend direction with statistical confidence. Co-occurrences between variables. Language themes from journals.

What it doesn't do: diagnose, suggest medication changes, or claim causality. Phrases like "this indicates," "you are depressed," or "reduce your dose" are explicitly blocked from all outputs. If you see language like that, it's a bug.

Minimum data requirements: the AI will not generate trend language from fewer than 7 check-ins, will not call a pattern statistically significant without p ≤ 0.05 and R² ≥ 0.25, and will not generate a summary if there are no check-ins or journals in the selected window. Sparse data is stated as sparse.

Missing fields are not zero. If a patient hasn't logged advanced check-in data, those fields are marked "not recorded" — not assumed to be zero. Absence of data is not the same as absence of the behavior.

The summaries and suggested questions are inputs to the clinical conversation. They give you a cleaner starting point — the interpretation is yours.
