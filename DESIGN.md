# Design System: CognaSync

## 1. Overview

**Creative North Star: "The Field Notebook"**

CognaSync looks like data kept by a thoughtful person, not generated by software. The aesthetic owes something to a well-worn researcher's notebook: warm paper tones, editorial serif type alongside precise monospaced numbers, ink-dark text on parchment surfaces. Every screen carries the sense that someone cared enough to record this carefully. The warmth is not decorative; it is what makes clinical data feel like it belongs to a person.

The system holds two audiences with different needs on the same visual plane. Patient surfaces breathe more, let the serif work, make space for the human dimension of tracking. Provider surfaces compress, structure, flag, and get out of the way. The design principles that govern both are identical: specificity earns trust, restraint signals rigor, precision is the point.

This system explicitly rejects the two failure modes it was built to avoid: the generic SaaS dashboard (hero metrics, identical card grids, gradient number callouts, navy sidebars) and the clinical EHR (cold white forms, sterile field grids, the thing that makes a patient feel like a record number). Neither the Consumer Wellness App (pastels, breathing animations, streak gamification) nor the Abstract Intelligence Platform (dark mode with neon, glassmorphism, floating UI) is this product.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Warm parchment surfaces with ink-dark text; teal reserved for actionable elements only
- DM Serif Display for names, scores, and section titles; DM Sans for everything else; JetBrains Mono for every number
- Structured shadow vocabulary: surfaces feel placed, not floating
- Tactile interactions: micro-feedback on press, opacity transitions on hover
- Status communicates through color + label together; color alone is never the signal
- No side-stripe borders, no gradient text, no SaaS hero-metric templates

## 2. Colors: The Parchment and Reed Palette

A warm parchment field with one committed organic accent. Teal is the only brand color; amber, red, and notation blue are semantic-only and never used decoratively.

### Primary
- **Reed Green** (`#0F6E56`): The single brand accent. Used on primary actions (buttons, active nav states, links), score displays, and status indicators. Appears on no more than 10-15% of any given screen.
- **Reed Mid** (`#1D9E75`): Lighter teal for hover states, tonal fills, and positive trend indicators.
- **Reed Light** (`#9FE1CB`): Tonal background for active states and step progress segments.
- **Reed Faint** (`#E1F5EE`): Tinted surface for teal-positive containers (stable badges, success states).

### Secondary (semantic: caution/watch)
- **Harvest Amber** (`#BA7517`): Watch-level status indicators. Never used on interactive elements. Never decorative.
- **Amber Faint** (`#FAEEDA`): Background for watch badges and caution containers.

### Tertiary (semantic: decline/crisis)
- **Signal Red** (`#E24B4A`): Decline trend badges, crisis banners, destructive actions. Reserved; its presence means something.
- **Red Faint** (`#FCEBEB`): Background for decline badges and danger containers.
- **Notation Blue** (`#185FA5`): Level-2 insight markers only. Never used as a brand color, never on navigation. A reference annotation, not an accent.

### Neutral
- **Ivory Card** (`#FAFAF8`): Card surfaces and elevated elements. The lightest surface; contains content.
- **Warm Parchment** (`#F4F2ED`): Page background. Warm, not white. Every neutral in this system is tinted toward the brand hue.
- **Faded Linen** (`#EDEBE6`): Inset surfaces, tertiary backgrounds, empty containers.
- **Vellum Border** (`#D3D1C7`): Standard borders and dividers.
- **Fieldnote Mist** (`#888780`): Tertiary text, placeholder text, hint labels.
- **Pencil Gray** (`#5F5E5A`): Secondary text, labels, supporting information.
- **Carbon Ink** (`#1A1A18`): Primary text. Near-black with a warm undertone; never pure `#000000`.

### Named Rules
**The Reed Rule.** Teal is the only brand color. Amber, red, and notation blue are semantic signals, not accent options. If a new element needs color and it is not communicating status, it uses teal or a neutral.

**The No-White Rule.** Neither `#fff` nor `#000` appears anywhere. Every surface is `#FAFAF8` or warmer. Every text is `#1A1A18` or lighter. The warmth is non-negotiable.

## 3. Typography: The Serif-Sans Pairing

**Display Font:** DM Serif Display (Georgia, serif fallback)
**Body Font:** DM Sans (system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif)
**Numeric/Mono Font:** JetBrains Mono (SF Mono, Courier New fallback)

**Character:** The pairing is editorial and precise. DM Serif Display carries names, scores, and section anchors with the authority of a printed heading. DM Sans handles everything operational: labels, body copy, navigation. JetBrains Mono is reserved exclusively for numbers (scores, timestamps, deltas), making data visually distinct from prose at a glance.

### Hierarchy
- **Display** (DM Serif Display, 400 weight, 40px, 1.1 lh, -0.02em tracking): Major score displays, hero section headings. The largest type in the system. Used sparingly.
- **Display Small** (DM Serif Display, 400 weight, 28px, 1.15 lh, -0.01em tracking): Section titles, patient names in provider context, pre-appointment summary headings.
- **Heading** (DM Serif Display, 400 weight, 20px, 1.2 lh): Card headings, page section anchors.
- **Subheading** (DM Sans, 500 weight, 15px, 1.3 lh): Widget headers, form group labels, navigation items.
- **Body** (DM Sans, 400 weight, 14px, 1.6 lh, max 68ch): Standard content, AI insight text, journal reflections. Line length capped at 65-75ch.
- **Label** (DM Sans, 500 weight, 12px, 1.5 lh): Supporting information, metadata, secondary labels.
- **Micro** (DM Sans, 500 weight, 10px, uppercase, 0.08em tracking): Status tags, category labels, badge text. All-caps only at this size.
- **Mono** (JetBrains Mono, 400 weight, 13px): Every numeric value in the interface. Scores, timestamps, deltas, statistical values. If it is a number, it is in Mono.

### Named Rules
**The Mono Number Rule.** Every score, delta, timestamp, or computed value renders in JetBrains Mono. No exceptions. The typographic distinction between "a number" and "a word" is the visual signal that data and prose are different kinds of truth.

**The Weight Ceiling Rule.** Maximum font weight is 500. No bold (600+) anywhere in the system. Hierarchy comes from scale contrast (≥1.25 ratio between steps), font-family contrast (serif vs. sans), and opacity — never from heaviness.

## 4. Elevation: Placed, Not Floating

Surfaces feel set onto the parchment background rather than hovering above it. The shadow vocabulary is structured and intentional: three named levels that establish clear reading hierarchy without making the interface feel like a Keynote slide deck.

Shadows are warm-tinted to stay coherent with the neutral palette. They are never pure-black shadows on a warm surface.

### Shadow Vocabulary
- **Resting** (`0 1px 2px rgba(26,26,24,0.05), 0 1px 3px rgba(26,26,24,0.04)`): Default card shadow. Present at rest. Distinguishes card from page background without asserting presence.
- **Lifted** (`0 2px 8px rgba(26,26,24,0.08), 0 4px 16px rgba(26,26,24,0.05)`): Hover state on interactive cards, focused input fields, sticky nav scroll state. State-responsive; appears on interaction.
- **Elevated** (`0 8px 32px rgba(26,26,24,0.12), 0 16px 48px rgba(26,26,24,0.06)`): Dropdowns, floating panels, modals when they are truly necessary. Reserved for elements that layer above the page.

### Named Rules
**The Warm Shadow Rule.** Shadows use `rgba(26,26,24, ...)` (Carbon Ink at opacity), not `rgba(0,0,0,...)`. A cold black shadow on a warm parchment surface reads as a mistake. The undertone must match.

**The State-Responsive Rule.** Cards carry the Resting shadow at rest. Lifted shadow appears only in response to hover, focus, or scroll state. Elevated shadow is reserved for elements that truly float above the page surface. Never apply Elevated shadow to a standard card.

## 5. Components

### Buttons
Tactile and responsive: micro-feedback on press, opacity fade on hover, no transforms that shift layout.
- **Shape:** Gently rounded (10px / `{rounded.md}`). The curves are present but not soft.
- **Primary:** Reed Green background (`#0F6E56`), Ivory Card text (`#FAFAF8`), 10px/18px padding. Font: DM Sans 500, 13px.
- **Hover:** Background shifts to Reed Deep (`#085041`). `opacity: 0.88` on hover as the universal hover signal — consistent across all button variants.
- **Active/Press:** `transform: scale(0.98)` — a felt click without layout shift.
- **Secondary:** Transparent background, Reed Green border (1px), Reed Green text. Same hover opacity rule.
- **Ghost:** Warm Parchment background, Pencil Gray text, 0.5px border in Vellum Border. Used for low-priority actions.
- **Danger:** Red Faint background, Signal Red border, deep red text. Destructive only.
- **Small variant:** 6px radius, 7px/13px padding, 12px font. Same color rules.
- **Focus:** `outline: 2px solid #1D9E75` at 2px offset. Visible and on-brand.

### Badges / Status Chips
Status chips communicate tier; they always carry a text label alongside any color signal.
- **Stable:** Reed Faint background (`#E1F5EE`), deep teal text (`#04342C`). For positive/stable status.
- **Watch:** Amber Faint background, deep amber text. For caution/watch status.
- **Decline:** Red Faint background, deep red text. For declining trend / alert status.
- **Neutral:** Faded Linen background, Pencil Gray text. For informational/unlabeled states.
- **Shape:** Pill (20px radius). 3px/9px padding. Micro typography (10px, 500, uppercase).

### Cards / Containers
- **Corner style:** 16px radius (`{rounded.lg}`) for full cards; 10px for compact variants.
- **Background:** Ivory Card (`#FAFAF8`) on the Warm Parchment page.
- **Shadow:** Resting shadow at rest; Lifted shadow on hover (interactive cards only).
- **Border:** 0.5px solid, opacity-based: `rgba(0,0,0,0.12)`. Hairline; present for definition, not decoration.
- **Internal padding:** 20px (`{spacing.5}`) for standard cards; 12px/16px for compact.
- **Nested cards:** Prohibited. Inset surfaces use the `surface-inset` (`#EDEBE6`) background tint instead.

### Inputs / Fields
- **Style:** Ivory Card background, 0.5px Vellum Border, 10px radius.
- **Focus:** Border shifts to Reed Green (`#0F6E56`) at 1.5px; Lifted shadow activates.
- **Placeholder text:** Fieldnote Mist (`#888780`).
- **Error state:** Signal Red border, Red Faint background tint. Error message in red below the field.
- **Disabled:** 0.4 opacity. Non-interactive state is visually quieted, not hidden.

### Navigation
- **Patient top nav:** Fixed, Ivory Card background, single 1px Carbon Ink border-bottom at scroll. 52px height.
- **Nav items:** DM Sans 500, 13-14px. Pencil Gray at rest; Carbon Ink on hover; Reed Green on active. No background pills on active state; color shift only.
- **Mobile:** Hamburger opens a full-width dropdown. Same item styles; 44px minimum touch target.
- **Overflow:** Patient nav has 11 items. On screens below 900px, items overflow into the hamburger menu rather than wrapping.

### Score Display (Signature Component)
The system's most distinctive element. Scores appear throughout patient and provider surfaces.
- **Hero score:** DM Serif Display 64px in Reed Green. Uppercase micro label above in Pencil Gray. Mono delta annotation below.
- **Score grid:** 2-column grid of compact score cells. Cell: Ivory Card surface, 10px radius, 0.5px border. Label in 10px Fieldnote Mist; value in JetBrains Mono 18px, 500; thin 2px progress bar at base.
- **Score bars:** 3px height, Faded Linen track, Reed Green fill. Never taller than 3px for a standard progress bar.
- **Never use the hero-metric template** (big number, small label, supporting stats, gradient accent). That is the SaaS cliché this system explicitly rejects. Scores are read as data, not celebrated as metrics.

### Insight Rows (Note: Pattern to Revise)
Current codebase uses `border-left: 2.5px solid` as a colored accent on insight items. This violates the side-stripe ban. The correct replacement is a background tint plus a leading label chip (using the existing badge component). Existing `.cs-insight` classes should be migrated to tinted-background rows with status badge labels.

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
- **Do** use `rgba(26,26,24, ...)` for all shadows. Warm tint, never pure black.
- **Do** render every numeric value in JetBrains Mono. Scores, timestamps, averages, deltas: all mono.
- **Do** use DM Serif Display for display headings, section anchors, and score labels. The serif is the Field Notebook's handwriting.
- **Do** pair every status color signal with a text label. Color alone is never the signal.
- **Do** apply `transform: scale(0.98)` on button active/press states. The interaction should feel tactile.
- **Do** use the three-level shadow vocabulary (`resting` / `lifted` / `elevated`) in their designated roles.
- **Do** keep body line length at 65-75ch. AI insight text and journal reflections must respect this constraint.
- **Do** use Faded Linen (`#EDEBE6`) tint for inset surfaces instead of nested cards.
- **Do** cap font weight at 500. Hierarchy comes from scale and family contrast, not bold weight.

### Don't:
- **Don't** use `border-left` or `border-right` greater than 1px as a colored stripe on any card, list item, or alert. The current `.cs-insight` side-stripe pattern is deprecated and must be migrated.
- **Don't** use generic SaaS dashboard patterns: hero-metric cards with big numbers, gradient accents, identical icon-heading-text card grids, or navy-sidebar chrome. CognaSync is not a KPI tracker.
- **Don't** make the interface feel like clinical EHR software (Epic, Cerner): no cold white form fields, no sterile field grids, no sea of labeled inputs without visual breathing room.
- **Don't** use gradient text (`background-clip: text` + gradient). Emphasis comes from DM Serif Display, scale contrast, or Reed Green color on a single token.
- **Don't** use `#000000` or `#ffffff` anywhere. Every surface is Ivory Card or warmer; every text is Carbon Ink or lighter.
- **Don't** apply semantic colors (amber, red, notation blue) decoratively. If an element uses Harvest Amber and it is not communicating a watch-level status, it is wrong.
- **Don't** use glassmorphism (backdrop-filter, frosted panels) anywhere. The Field Notebook does not blur its pages.
- **Don't** nest cards. If a surface inside a card needs separation, use the Faded Linen inset tint.
- **Don't** surface any patient-facing crisis or safety flag in provider-only color treatments or the reverse. The two surfaces serve different audiences with different emotional contexts.
- **Don't** use font weight above 500. Bold text in a clinical tool looks like an alert, not a heading.
