The developers of the Mental Fitness Vocal Biomarker (MFVB) algorithm opted for a simple averaging approach rather than machine learning because they believe this simplicity makes the algorithm more robust and generalizable across different populations 1\.  
By using a straightforward method of normalizing and averaging specific vocal features against a reference database, the algorithm successfully avoided overfitting to a single demographic 1\. For instance, the MFVB was calibrated using a large biobank from outpatient clinics across India, but it remained effective when validated on a distinctly different cohort of patients in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania 1, 2\.  
However, the researchers do not plan to avoid machine learning permanently. They note that the success of their simple algorithm provides a strong foundation, and future versions of the MFVB will likely incorporate machine learning and artificial intelligence to further optimize the scores and improve their alignment with mental health symptoms 1\.
