The eight vocal features extracted and analyzed to generate the Mental Fitness Vocal Biomarker (MFVB) score are:

* **Jitter:** The variation in the time between consecutive pitch periods, which relates to a speaker's vocal cord control 1\.  
* **Shimmer:** The variation in the amplitude of consecutive pitch periods, which is also a measure of vocal cord control 2\.  
* **Pitch variability:** The intentional variation in voice pitch used for intonation, which is driven by higher-level cognitive processes 2\.  
* **Energy variability:** The intentional variation in the energy or intensity of the voice used for emphasis 2\.  
* **Vowel space:** The separation between the frequencies of the first two formants, which indicates the coordination of vocal tract articulators 2\.  
* **Phonation duration:** The average duration from the onset to the offset of phonation (glottal vibration) 3\.  
* **Speech rate:** The number of words spoken per minute 3\.  
* **Pause duration:** The median duration of gaps between voice activity 3\.

These specific features were selected for the MFVB algorithm because published research has established their correlation with depression and other mental health conditions 3, 4\.
