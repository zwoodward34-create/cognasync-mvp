The Mental Fitness Vocal Biomarker (MFVB) and the M3 Checklist are strongly aligned, with MFVB scores successfully acting as an objective indicator of the self-reported symptom severities measured by the M3 Checklist 1, 2\.  
Here is how the two compare in detail:  
**The Measurement Scales**

* **The M3 Checklist** is a validated, patient-reported survey that measures mental health symptom severity across four domains: depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), and bipolar disorder 3, 4\. It categorizes a patient's overall symptom severity as either normal, mild, moderate, or severe (with moderate and severe grouped together as "elevated" symptoms) 4, 5\.  
* **The MFVB** outputs a score from 0 to 100 based on an analysis of eight vocal features, which is then grouped into three categories: "Excellent" (80-100), "Good" (70-79), and "Pay Attention" (0-69) 6-8.

**How the Scores Align**Studies show that as a user's MFVB score category moves from "Excellent" down to "Pay Attention," their M3 total symptom scores correspondingly increase 1\. To quantify this comparison, researchers calculated a **Relative Risk Ratio (RR Ratio)** to measure how much more likely someone with a "Pay Attention" MFVB score is to have elevated M3 symptoms compared to someone with an "Excellent" MFVB score 2, 9, 10\.

* **Single Reading (Closest-MFVB):** When comparing a single 30-second voice recording to the M3 Checklist, the RR Ratio was **1.53** 2, 11\.  
* **Continuous Tracking (Time-Weighted):** When aggregating a user's MFVB scores over a two-week period and comparing them to the M3 Checklist, the RR Ratio improved to **2.00** 2, 11\. The correlation became even stronger (an RR Ratio of 8.50) for highly engaged users who used the MFVB app five to six times a week 12, 13\.

**Performance Across Specific M3 Domains**When breaking down the M3 Checklist into its specific sub-domains, the MFVB's alignment is most pronounced for **depression** 14\. For depression symptoms alone, the RR Ratio was 1.78 for a single voice sample and jumped to 2.60 when using the time-weighted two-week average 15\. The MFVB also showed consistent relative risk trends for anxiety and PTSD, but evaluating its alignment with the M3's bipolar disorder metrics proved challenging due to a limited number of elevated bipolar cases in the study data 16\.
