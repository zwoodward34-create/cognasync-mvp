**No, the Mental Fitness Vocal Biomarker (MFVB) score does not use machine learning models in any part of its scoring algorithm** 1\.  
Instead of relying on machine learning, the MFVB utilizes a **straightforward averaging approach** based on a simple combination of pre-selected voice acoustic features 1, 2\. The process works as follows:

* The system extracts **eight specific vocal features** that are well-supported in literature as being relevant to mental health, such as jitter, pitch variability, speech rate, and pause duration 3, 4\.  
* The raw values of these extracted features are **normalized against distributions from a large reference database** (a proprietary digital biobank compiled from clinical studies across multiple hospital sites in India) 1, 5\.  
* Once normalized, these values are **averaged together to generate a single composite score** ranging from 0 to 100, where a higher score indicates greater mental fitness (a lower likelihood of elevated mental health symptoms) 1\.

Researchers suggest that the simplicity of the current algorithm is likely what makes it so robust and generalizable across different populations 2, 5\. However, the success of this straightforward scoring method provides an encouraging foundation, and **future versions of the MFVB algorithm may incorporate machine learning or artificial intelligence** to further optimize the scores and improve their alignment with mental health symptoms 5\.
