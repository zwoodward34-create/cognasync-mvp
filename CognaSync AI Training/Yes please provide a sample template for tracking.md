## What to track

For most psychiatric care, the most useful items are:

- Mood.
- Anxiety.
- Sleep.
- Energy.
- Medication adherence.
- Side effects.
- Stressors or triggers.
- Daily functioning.
- Any safety concerns.


## Simple example entry

**Date:** May 31
**Sleep:** 6.5 hours | Quality 4/10
**Mood:** 5/10
**Anxiety:** 7/10
**Energy:** 4/10
**Medication taken as prescribed?** Yes
**Side effects:** Mild morning grogginess.
**Major stressors or triggers:** Work deadline, poor sleep.
**Functioning today:** Got through work but avoided two meetings.
**Notes:** Felt better after a walk.

## Best way to use it

Keep it brief and consistent. A psychiatrist usually gets more value from 1–2 minutes of daily tracking than from long detailed notes, because patterns are easier to spot across time.

A good rule is: track enough to show trends, not every thought. If you want, I can turn this into a **fill-in-the-blank weekly form** or a **spreadsheet layout** next.
