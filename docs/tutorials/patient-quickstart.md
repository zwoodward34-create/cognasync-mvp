# CognaSync Patient Quick-Start Guide

CognaSync tracks what you do and how you feel between appointments, then turns that data into plain-language patterns you and your provider can talk about. It doesn't diagnose, prescribe, or give medical advice — it just keeps an honest record so your appointments start from the same page.

This guide covers everything you need for your first week.

---

## Step 1: Complete your first check-in

The daily check-in is the core habit. From the home screen, tap **Check In** to open the form.

You'll be asked to log:

- **Mood** (0–10) — how you're feeling overall right now
- **Stress** (0–10) — how much pressure or tension you're carrying
- **Sleep** — hours slept last night
- **Medications** — which ones you took today, and roughly when
- **Notes** — anything you want your provider to know about today

You can do check-ins in the morning, afternoon, or evening — each one captures a snapshot of that part of your day. If you only do one, once a day is enough to start.

**After you submit**, you'll see a brief AI reflection: a sentence or two that references your actual numbers. It's not a diagnosis — it's just a plain-language read of what you logged. You can rate it with a thumbs up or down; that feedback helps the system improve.

> **Advanced check-in fields** — once you're comfortable with the basics, you can unlock fields for energy, focus, caffeine, exercise, and more. These give your provider a fuller picture but aren't required.

---

## Step 2: Write your first journal entry

From the nav, tap **Journal**. Write whatever's on your mind — there's no required format. A sentence is fine. A few paragraphs is fine too.

A few things to know:

- **Journal entries are shared with your provider by default.** If you want to keep an entry private, toggle off "Share with provider" before saving.
- After you save, you'll see an AI reflection — a brief observation about what you wrote. It will reflect themes and language from the entry without interpreting what it means clinically.
- If your entry contains language that suggests you may be in crisis, CognaSync will skip the reflection and show you crisis resources instead. Your entry is still saved.

---

## Step 3: Set up your medication list

Go to **Settings → Medications** to add the medications you take regularly. For each one, enter the name and dose.

Once your medications are in the system, two things get easier:

- **During check-ins**, your medications appear in the form so you can mark them taken or missed with one tap.
- **On the home screen**, the medication widget lets you quick-log a dose with a single tap — no need to open a full check-in.

To log a dose from the home screen: tap the medication name, confirm the time, and it's recorded. You can undo an accidental log by tapping it again.

---

## Step 4: Link your provider

For your provider to see your data, you need to link your accounts.

Go to **Settings → Provider** and enter your provider's CognaSync email address. Once linked, they'll have read access to your check-ins, shared journal entries, scores, and trends. They cannot edit your data.

If you don't know your provider's email, ask them — they'll need to have a CognaSync provider account first.

You can unlink at any time from the same settings screen.

---

## Step 5: Generate a pre-appointment summary

A few days before your next appointment, go to the **Summaries** page and tap **Generate Summary**.

The summary covers the past 14 days (you can adjust the window) and includes:

- How your mood, sleep, and stress trended
- What stood out compared to your baseline
- Themes from your journal entries
- 2–3 suggested topics to bring to your appointment

Read it before you go in. It's written for you, not your provider — plain language, your data, framed around what you might want to discuss. Your provider generates their own version on their end with more clinical detail.

---

## Quick reference: what the scores mean

After each check-in, CognaSync computes a few summary scores. Here's what they represent in plain terms:

| Score | What it reflects |
|---|---|
| **Stability Score** | How aligned your mood, energy, and anxiety signals are. Higher = more consistent. |
| **Stim Load** | Total stimulant input for the day — caffeine tiers plus any stimulant medications. A high Stim Load on a bad sleep day is worth noting. |
| **Crash Risk** | A combined read of sleep disruption, nervous system strain, and nutrition. High Crash Risk days are useful context for your provider. |
| **Nervous System Load** | How much strain your anxiety, sleep quality, and stimulant intake are putting on your system together. |

None of these scores is a diagnosis. They're inputs — things to notice, track, and talk about.

## If you need help right now

If you're struggling and need to talk to someone:

- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** — call or text **988**
- **Crisis Text Line** — text HOME to **741741**
- **Emergency** — call **911**
